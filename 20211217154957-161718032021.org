The distributive laws in propositional logic say that for any propositions $P$, $Q$ and $R$

$$
\begin{align}
\text{(1) } P\land (Q\lor R) \text{ is equivalent to } (P\land Q)\lor (P\land R); \\
\text{(2) } P\lor (Q\land R) \text{ is equivalent to } (P\lor Q)\land (P\lor R).
\end{align}
$$

The truth table for the proof of (1) is the following.

$P$| $Q$ |$R$| $P\land (Q\lor R)$ | $(P\land Q)\lor (P\land Q)$
